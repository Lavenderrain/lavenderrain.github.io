Zero Waste

Nike is a leading spotswear company consuming a Zero waste in production turning its own trash into treasure.

Nike's work to reduce waste and move toward a closed-loop model is Nike Grind, a palette of premium recycled and regenerated materials created from original materials and products.

TECHNOLOGY

Nike technology makes the recycling materials are derived from old shoes, plastic bottles and factory scraps branded Nike Grind.

Through a "slice-and-grind" technique, shoes are split into three sections- separating the rubber from the outsole, foam from the midsole and fiber from the upper sole-before they are put through a grinder and transformed into fabric pellets for future use in another pair of shoes, a track court, a playground or another athletic padding surface.

Nike Grind materials are used in 71% of Nike footwear and apparel products, in everything from apparel trims to soccer kits to Flyknit yarns.

Innovation

NIKE has revolutionized sustainability in performance product. NIKE's vision is closed loop, which takes materials from an old shoe or an old shirt, grinds them up and transforms them into new products.

Continuously exploring ways to incorporate sustainability has led Nike to highlight two groundbreaking innovations.

NIKE Flyknit and the expanded use of recycled polyester in performance apparel. innovation is in the NIKE Flyknit manufacturing process; NIKE uses only the materials needed to knit the upper of a shoe. Traditional footwear manufacturing processes use a number of materials and cuts.

NIKE Flyknit reduces waste using special yarns, knitted together to create one lightweight, formfitting upper.
